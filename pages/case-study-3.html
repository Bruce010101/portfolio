BAK (Hackaton project)
project

bak 2021

summary

A 72 hour marathon, where the goal was to create
a brand presence and a tidy user-friendly website
that displays all the features the company offers.

role
ux/ui designer

problem definition

This project (BAK) was the biggest cross academy projects organized by Brainster. We worked in a team of 2 UX/UI designers, 3 Graphic designers & 2 Digital Marketers. The Project started with a brief from our client. He explained to us his goal and vision. We gained enough information and started to work asap.

research

After the briefing session, we stated with coordinating the 3 teams to establish a good communication and work environment. After we got to know each other more and divided the tasks to all the teams accordingly. Therefore, we conducted that the user wanted to a whole presence on the internet given the fact that he is new to the logistics world, he also wanted a fully responsive web site, so that he can showcase the work, clients and services that he provides. Also, we determined the must have functionalities.

user interviews

The main conclusion that we received from the stakeholder interview is that we could expand our vision & creativity for the solutions on the problem that we needed to solve. The client (BAK Logistics) gave us the task to design a brand-focused platform where their clients can learn more about the services that they offer. The website needs to exhibit the following values: reliable, trustworthy, secure. They need to have a good online presence, in order for clients to find them more easily.

analytics

The Analytics provided us with information regarding the design. Overall, the online presence is a must and mobile first design approach was needed and the landing page to be good, with an animated hero section. We researched the logistics market to find out the must have functionalities.

solution

After the analysis of the competitors, user interviews, target groups the solution would be create a website that can stand out as much as possible, make it intuitive and user-friendly, showcase all the services that the company offers, and make it as easy to use as possible.

outcome

In 72 hours with a lot of focus and commitment, very little sleep and overall good team work from all 3 teams we managed to create a fully responsive website with a corporate yet “wow” design. As the saying goes” The devil is in the details”.

learnings

This project alone was the best lesson & experience that I had throughout the whole academy. It thought me new ways to collaborate with other teams and also upgrade my team work capabilities. It has increased my understanding of the role of a UX/UI designer. Overall good communication is key between different departments, and it reflects on the final product.
